Human Hormones: A Complete Guide

The human body produces a wide variety of hormones, which are chemical messengers that regulate growth, metabolism, mood, reproduction, and other vital functions. Below is a detailed list of all major hormones, their sources, functions, and stimuli for release.

Adrenaline (Epinephrine)

Source: Adrenal glands (medulla)

Function: Triggers the "fight-or-flight" response by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy availability.

Release Stimuli: Stressful situations, danger, excitement, or intense physical activity.

Noradrenaline (Norepinephrine)

Source: Adrenal glands (medulla) and nerve endings

Function: Works with adrenaline to enhance the "fight-or-flight" response; helps maintain blood pressure and focus.

Release Stimuli: Acute stress, fear, or low blood sugar.

Back to top

Cortisol

Source: Adrenal glands (cortex)

Function: Helps manage stress, regulate metabolism, and control inflammation.

Release Stimuli: Chronic stress, low blood sugar, or morning wake-up.

Back to top

Insulin

Source: Pancreas (beta cells)

Function: Lowers blood sugar by allowing cells to absorb glucose for energy or storage.

Release Stimuli: High blood sugar levels (e.g., after eating).

Back to top

Glucagon

Source: Pancreas (alpha cells)

Function: Raises blood sugar by stimulating the liver to release glucose.

Release Stimuli: Low blood sugar levels or prolonged physical activity.

Back to top

Thyroxine (T4) and Triiodothyronine (T3)

Source: Thyroid gland

Function: Regulate metabolism, energy production, and growth.

Release Stimuli: Thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) from the pituitary gland.

Back to top

Growth Hormone (GH)

Source: Pituitary gland (anterior)

Function: Stimulates growth, muscle mass development, and fat metabolism.

Release Stimuli: Exercise, sleep, stress, or low blood sugar.

Back to top

Prolactin

Source: Pituitary gland (anterior)

Function: Promotes milk production in breastfeeding mothers.

Release Stimuli: Pregnancy and breastfeeding, emotional bonding with a baby.

Back to top

Oxytocin

Source: Hypothalamus (released by the posterior pituitary)

Function: Facilitates bonding, trust, and labor contractions.

Release Stimuli: Physical touch, childbirth, or breastfeeding.

Back to top

Melatonin

Source: Pineal gland

Function: Regulates sleep-wake cycles (circadian rhythm).

Release Stimuli: Darkness (low light levels).

Back to top

Estrogen

Source: Ovaries (in females), small amounts in adrenal glands (males and females)

Function: Regulates menstrual cycles, supports pregnancy, and maintains bone health.

Release Stimuli: Luteinizing hormone (LH) from the pituitary gland.

Back to top

Progesterone

Source: Ovaries (corpus luteum) and placenta (during pregnancy)

Function: Prepares the uterus for pregnancy and supports fetal development.

Release Stimuli: Ovulation and pregnancy.

Back to top

Testosterone

Source: Testes (in males), small amounts in ovaries and adrenal glands (females)

Function: Promotes muscle mass, libido, and male secondary sexual characteristics.

Release Stimuli: Luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH).

Back to top

Follicle-Stimulating Hormone (FSH)

Source: Pituitary gland (anterior)

Function: Stimulates egg production (in females) and sperm production (in males).

Release Stimuli: Gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) from the hypothalamus.

Back to top

Luteinizing Hormone (LH)

Source: Pituitary gland (anterior)

Function: Triggers ovulation and testosterone production.

Release Stimuli: GnRH from the hypothalamus.

Back to top

Vasopressin (Antidiuretic Hormone, ADH)

Source: Hypothalamus (released by the posterior pituitary)

Function: Regulates water balance by controlling urine concentration.

Release Stimuli: Dehydration, high blood osmolality (concentrated blood).

Back to top

Parathyroid Hormone (PTH)

Source: Parathyroid glands

Function: Increases calcium levels in the blood by stimulating bone breakdown.

Release Stimuli: Low blood calcium levels.

Back to top

Calcitonin

Source: Thyroid gland

Function: Lowers blood calcium levels by promoting calcium storage in bones.

Release Stimuli: High blood calcium levels.

Back to top

Leptin

Source: Fat cells (adipose tissue)

Function: Regulates hunger and energy balance by signaling satiety.

Release Stimuli: Increased fat storage.

Back to top

Ghrelin

Source: Stomach

Function: Stimulates hunger and food intake.

Release Stimuli: Empty stomach.

Back to top

Serotonin

Source: Intestines and brain

Function: Regulates mood, appetite, and sleep.

Release Stimuli: Exposure to sunlight or consuming foods rich in tryptophan (e.g., turkey, eggs).

Back to top

Dopamine

Source: Brain (produced in the substantia nigra and hypothalamus)

Function: Regulates reward, motivation, and mood.

Release Stimuli: Pleasurable activities (e.g., eating, exercise) or achieving goals.

Back to top
